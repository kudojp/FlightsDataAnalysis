by Daiki Kudo¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the arrival delay of the domestic flights of US in 2008. Arrival delay can be broken down into Departure Delay and Elapsed Time Delay. Elaped Time Delay is the lag between Actual Elapsed Time and CRS(scheduled) Elapsed Time. This means how many minutes passengers had to spend their extra time in the airplane. I decided to investigate these 2 delays associated with 4 factors( "CarrierDelay", "WeatherDelay", "NASDelay",and "LateAircraftDelay") and clarify the features which can be found in the long delay.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consists of approximately 7 million domestic records in United States. This records include date, distance, arrival time and other various features such as delay factors. I made "Through the analysis, around 150000 flight data was removed. They are the flights which are canceled or diverted as well as the those whose origin or destination is in Hawaii.

Distribution of Arrival Delay¶

Arrival delay in the dataset takes on a very large range of values, most of them are from about -60 to about 240 minutes. Negative value means the plane arrived earlier than scheduled. The mode of the distribution is at around 0 minute, as is easily expected.

Attributes of 4 Delay Factors (1. Occurence Rates)¶

NSA Delay was the most prevalent delay factor in 2008 and it happened around once every 7 flights. The Late Aircraft Delay and Carrier Delay followed with around 10 % each. The Weather Delay rarely happened and the occurence rate was below 2%.
